WHAT WE DID

EVHS Robotics

Team 2854 has founded, and mentored, 3 award-winning FTC teams at EVHS.

FTC Athena 9657

  • The first FTC team we founded was Athena, in 2014.
  • Athena is an all-girls team, committed to breaking gender barriers in STEM and empowering women.
  • When it started, there were only 4 girls on the team. Today, there are over 20 members!
  • Athena has won a total of 14 awards, including the Connect Award (4x), the Think Award (2x), and Control Award (2x).
View Team

FTC Artemis 18715

  • FTC Artemis was founded in 2020, during the COVID-19 pandemic.
  • Despite the challenges of building a robot remotely, they had a successful rookie season, winning 3 awards.
  • They have won a total of 20 awards, including the Inspire Award (3x), Motivate Award (5x), and went to the World Championship in Houston in 2022.
View Team

FTC Kronos 20791

  • FTC Kronos is our newest team, founded in 2021.
  • Despite their short lifespan, Kronos has made a huge impact in the community.
  • They have won 6 awards, including the Motivate Award (2x), the Connect Award, and the Control Award.

Prototypes Around The World

FRC 6520 GreenAms Robotics Team

  • We created the first branch of the Society of Women Engineers (SWE) in Vietnam by collaborating with the first and only Vietnam FRC team 6520.
  • With FRC 6520, we hosted meetings to spread inclusivity in STEM.
  • We guided 6250 in preparing for their competition in Australia-Southern Cross Regional.
View Team

FTC 21300 Robots in a Nutshell (RIAN)

  • We started the first-ever FTC team in Vietnam and provided mentorship to spread FIRST.
  • We reached out to FIRST Secured and helped them get a spot at FTC regional in India.

Women in STEM Speaker Event

  • We invited a Stanford alumni/lecturer, and a few Berkeley and SJSU students to join us and spread awareness about the urgent issue of diversity in STEM.
  • This event reached over 200 students and inspired and impacted the community of women in STEM at Evergreen Valley High School.
  • We collaborated with Artemis (FTC team founded by us) to host this event.

Community Outreach

  • Our community outreach consisted of several workshops for kids ages 5-15, teaching and inspiring them in engineering and programming, creating a community for children to become the best they can be in their STEM journey.
  • We have successfully partnered with SpectroCloud, a local STEM company, and held weekly development meetings with our team and guest speakers.
  • These events spark innovation and passion throughout our team and our community.
